## The First Question: Silk or Silk Blend?

For South Indian weddings, a pure Kanchipuram silk is the traditional choice — the weight, sheen, and zari work are unmatched. However, for summer weddings or brides who want something lighter, a georgette-silk or crepe-silk blend may be more comfortable through a long ceremony.

## Choosing Your Colour

The bridal colour palette in South India tends toward vivid reds, deep maroons, and auspicious oranges — but modern brides are increasingly choosing rich royal blues, emerald greens, and ivory golds. Our stylists recommend choosing a colour that complements your skin tone rather than following trends blindly.

- **Deep skin tones** glow in jewel tones — royal blue, deep bottle green, rich maroon
- **Medium skin tones** suit most colours — consider burnt orange, teal, or rich pink  
- **Fair skin tones** look stunning in bold reds, purples, and deep navy

## The Zari Weight

Zari weight determines how "heavy" the saree feels and how dramatically it photographs. Our bridal collection ranges from medium zari (elegant and comfortable for long ceremonies) to heavy zari (maximum impact for wedding photography and reception events).
