## Why Silk Needs Special Care

Silk is a natural protein fibre — just like your hair. It is inherently delicate and reactive to chemicals, moisture, and sunlight. A saree that is improperly stored or washed can lose its lustre in months. A properly cared-for silk saree will shine brighter decades from now.

## Washing Rules

**Never machine-wash a pure silk saree.** The agitation of a washing machine will damage the delicate fibres and destroy the zari work permanently.

- Use cool water only
- Use a mild, pH-neutral soap or specialist silk wash
- Never wring or twist — gently press water out and hang to dry in shade
- Avoid direct sunlight — UV rays fade natural silk dyes rapidly

## Dry Cleaning

For heavily zari-worked pieces (especially our bridal collection), dry cleaning is always the safest option. Inform your dry cleaner specifically that the saree contains pure zari so they use appropriate solvents.

## Storage — The Most Important Step

- **Wrap in muslin cloth** — never plastic. Plastic traps moisture and promotes mould.
- **Store flat or rolled**, not folded on sharp creases (fold lines weaken silk fibres over years).
- **Add neem leaves or cedar blocks** — natural insect repellents that won't damage the fibres.
- **Air your sarees every 3-4 months**, ideally on a cool, breezy day in the shade.

## Zari Care

Always fold your saree with the zari on the inside to prevent tarnishing from exposure to air. If the zari has tarnished, a gentle buffing with a dry soft cloth can restore some shine.

Your Sumathi saree is built to last a lifetime and beyond. Treat it with the care it deserves.
