## Where It Begins — The Raw Silk

Every saree at Sumathi Textiles starts its life in the silkworm farms of Karnataka. We source exclusively from ethical sericulture farms, ensuring only Grade-A mulberry silk cocoons make it into our looms. The filament from a single cocoon can stretch up to 900 metres — and it takes approximately 6,000 cocoons to produce just one silk saree.

## Setting Up the Loom — A One-Week Task

Before a single thread is woven, two master artisans spend an entire week setting up the traditional pit loom. This involves hand-threading thousands of individual warp threads — each one requiring precise positioning. A single mistake here would create a visible flaw that cannot be corrected later.

## The Korvai Technique — What Makes Kanchipuram Unique

What separates a genuine Kanchipuram from all other silks is the Korvai technique, where the body and the border are woven as two completely separate silk pieces and interlocked during weaving. This creates the distinctive contrast between the body colour and the border — and it is the reason Kanchipuram sarees are so incredibly durable. The border will never detach.

## Zari Work — Pure Gold on Silk

Our signature sarees use pure zari thread — fine silver wires coated in real gold or silver. Each zari motif is woven by hand using a technique passed down across generations. Complex peacock, lotus, and temple patterns can require the weaver to make over 10,000 individual shuttle passes.

## 40 Days Later — A Legacy Piece

From the first thread to the final finishing inspection, creating a single signature Kanchipuram saree at Sumathi takes between 30 and 45 days. This is not mass production — it is living heritage, worn by generation after generation.

When you drape a Sumathi saree, you carry four decades of craftsmanship on your shoulders.
